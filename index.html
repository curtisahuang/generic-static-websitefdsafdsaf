What is a “flapjack”?

“Flapjack” means different things depending on where you are:

UK: Oat-Bar Flapjack

A chewy, buttery tray-bake made from oats, butter, brown sugar, and golden syrup (or honey).

US: Pancake “Flapjack”

A fluffy griddled pancake, often served in stacks with butter and maple syrup. Light and airy inside.

Recipes

Classic UK Flapjack (Oat Bar)

Prep 10 min • Bake 20–25 min • Makes 12 bars

Ingredients

  • 200 g unsalted butter
  • 150 g light brown sugar
  • 150 g golden syrup (or honey)
  • 350 g rolled oats
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: 1 tsp vanilla, handful of dried fruit or chocolate chips

Method

  1. Heat oven to 180°C (160°C fan) / 350°F. Line a 20×20 cm (8×8 in) tin with parchment.
  2. Melt butter, sugar and syrup together in a saucepan over low heat; stir until dissolved.
  3. Off heat, stir in oats and salt until evenly coated. Fold in any optional add-ins.
  4. Press mixture into tin. Bake 20–25 min until edges are golden.
  5. Cool in tin. Slice into bars while slightly warm for cleaner edges.

For chewier bars, bake less; for crisper ones, bake a bit longer.

Buttermilk Pancakes (US “Flapjacks”)

Prep 10 min • Cook 15 min • Serves 3–4

Ingredients

  • 240 g (2 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp fine salt
  • 480 ml (2 cups) buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 45 g (3 tbsp) melted butter, plus more for the pan
  • Optional: 1 tsp vanilla

Method

  1. Whisk dry ingredients in a bowl. In another, whisk buttermilk, eggs, and melted butter.
  2. Combine wet into dry, stirring just until flour disappears (lumpy batter is fine).
  3. Heat a lightly buttered skillet over medium. Pour 1/4 cup portions.
  4. Cook until bubbles form and edges set; flip and cook until golden.
  5. Serve with butter and warm maple syrup. Add berries or chocolate chips if you like.

Resting the batter 5–10 minutes improves fluffiness.

Tips

  • Use rolled oats (not instant) for UK flapjacks; they hold their bite.
  • Golden syrup gives classic flavor; honey works but is less toffee‑like.
  • For pancakes, don’t overmix. Small lumps prevent toughness.
  • Control heat: medium for even browning without burning.
  • Weigh ingredients for consistency.
