What is a smash burger?

A smash burger is a loose ball of ground beef pressed hard onto a ripping‑hot griddle. The firm press creates maximum contact for a deep, lacy Maillard crust while keeping the interior juicy and tender.

Why smash?

Thin patties brown fast, giving huge flavor from the crust. Short cook times keep the beef juicy with irresistible, crisp edges.

Essential gear

Heavy cast‑iron or steel griddle, a stiff metal spatula (sharp edge), parchment squares, 80/20 ground beef, soft potato buns, and American cheese.

Recipes

Classic Smashburger

Prep 10 min • Cook 10 min • Makes 2 doubles

Ingredients

  • 360–400 g 80/20 ground beef (4 × 90–100 g balls)
  • Fine salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 slices American cheese
  • 2 soft burger buns, buttered
  • 1–2 tsp neutral oil or beef tallow
  • Optional: dill pickles, thin onion, shredded lettuce, burger sauce or mustard

Method

  1. Preheat a cast‑iron skillet/griddle over medium‑high until lightly smoking (5–8 min).
  2. Form loose beef balls; do not compress. Season only after smashing.
  3. Toast buns in a little butter; set aside.
  4. Lightly oil the griddle. Place 2 beef balls, top with parchment, and smash hard 10–15 s to ~6 mm (1/4 in) thick. Remove parchment; season.
  5. Cook 60–90 s until edges are deeply browned. Scrape under the crust with a thin spatula and flip in one motion.
  6. Top each patty with cheese; cook 30–45 s more. Stack two patties per bun.
  7. Dress with pickles/onion and sauce. Serve immediately.

Smash once only in the first 10–15 seconds—pressing later squeezes out juices.

Oklahoma Onion Smashburger

Prep 10 min • Cook 10 min • Serves 2

Ingredients

  • 2 small onions, shaved paper‑thin
  • 360–400 g 80/20 ground beef (4 × 90–100 g balls)
  • Fine salt and black pepper
  • 4 slices American cheese
  • 2 soft burger buns, buttered
  • Neutral oil or beef tallow

Method

  1. Preheat griddle over medium‑high. Lightly salt the onions and toss.
  2. Place beef balls on the hot surface. Pile a mound of onions on each, top with parchment, and smash hard to 1/4 in.
  3. Season. Cook until edges brown; flip so onions contact the griddle. Tuck stray onions back under the patty.
  4. Top with cheese and cook 30–45 s more. Toast buns and assemble.
  5. Optional: add a splash of water and cover briefly to steam‑melt the cheese.

Shave onions very thin so they soften quickly without burning.

Tips

  • Use 80/20 beef—fat helps brown and keeps patties juicy.
  • Preheat the pan thoroughly; you want instant sizzle on contact.
  • Smash once, in the first 10–15 seconds; do not press again.
  • Use parchment when smashing to prevent sticking to the spatula.
  • Scrape with a sharp‑edged spatula to keep the crust intact; toast buns to resist moisture.
